TLDR

At Frontier Communications, I enabled powerful analyses of disparate datasets by developing and maintaining complex cloud-based ETL pipelines. Subsequently, I built robust, predictive models and dashboards to empower network operations teams’ responses to customer service issues.

Description

Though exact details are proprietary, I will highlight the breadth of the work that I’ve performed at Frontier Communcations. For context, Frontier Communications is a large-sized telecommunications company, aiming to provide fiber and copper service across the United States. In my current role, I work on the network analytics team, harnessing network data (i.e., bit rates, outages) to push data-driven decisions for the operations teams.

My biggest challenge at Frontier involved growing the company’s data maturity. Frontier, as an organization, had gathered a veritable gold mine of data by capturing automated responses from millions of network devices across the country (the size of one of the largest datasets was over a petabyte). However, I joined the company at a time when the tools to wrangle said data were underdeveloped. As such, I contributed to developing the architecture and protocols for extracting, processing, and analyzing data. The resulting data pipeline accelerated my ability to work with and analyze many internal datasets.

Being on the network team, reducing outages and optimizing network performance were a main driver for my projects. In one of my largest projects, I deployed a full-scale anomaly detection model, identifying several thousand customers that have recently experienced poor network service. I remember struggling with the many different ways to optimize the model performance. For example, I rigorously tested and reflected on the benefits and drawbacks of various outlier detection models (i.e., one-class SVMs, CBLOF, isolation forests, autoencoders) and ultimately made the most informed decision based on my sample analyses.

Additionally, here is a short list of my main responsbilities and achievements:

  • Constructed numerous ETL pipelines by leveraging cloud-based services and data engineering practices
  • Compiled, cleaned, and processed various data sets stored across multiple platforms to generate a large-scale, standardized data lake used widely among cross-functional network teams
  • Swiftly delivered ad-hoc key analyses and insights to network teams
  • Developed machine learning models to detect anomalies in service among copper DSL customers
